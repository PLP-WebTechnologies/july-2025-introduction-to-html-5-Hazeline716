Why Semantic HTML Matters

Semantic HTML elements, such as `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<article>`, `<section>`, and `<footer>`, provide meaning to web content. They are crucial for accessibility and search engine optimization (SEO).

Accessibility Benefits

For users relying on screen readers or other assistive technologies, semantic tags provide a roadmap of the page. A screen reader can announce a `<nav>` element as a navigation menu or a `<h2>` as a new section heading, making the content far more understandable.

Key Tags:

  • <nav> for navigation links.
  • <main> for the primary content of the document.
  • <figure> and <figcaption> for images with captions.

SEO Benefits

Search engines like Google use semantic tags to understand the context and hierarchy of a page's content. A well-structured page helps them index your content more effectively, which can lead to better search rankings.
